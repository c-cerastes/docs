Abstract

This study details a structured multiple linear regression model building process, applied to a chemical dataset to predict the concentration of NbOCl3NbOCl_3NbOCl3​ given the inclusion of 444 variables. The process began with a comprehensive model including all predictors, which was then methodically refined through statistical diagnostics and iterative all regression computation to enhance its predictive power or reduce complexity. The culmination is a theoretical model derived from the chemical rate equation, exhibiting high predictive accuracy and a strong adherence to the assumptions of least squares regression. Despite an indication of high collinearity, the model's robustness is supported by its theoretical underpinnings. Future iterations of the model will incorporate temperature as a variable, guided by the Arrhenius equation, to capture its effect on reaction rates.

Introduction

Regression analysis serves as a fundamental analytical tool in chemical engineering, allowing for the investigation of variable relationships within complex chemical systems. This paper presents an application of the standard model building process for regression as applied to the analysis of a dataset detailing a chemical reaction. The research question at the heart of this study investigates which regression model most accurately describes the relationship between the concentrations of reactants and products. Through iterative model building, three main classes of models have emerged as candidates that will be investigated.

The significance of identifying the most fitting model lies in its ability to reveal the underlying relationships that govern the concentrations of reactants and products. Such insights are instrumental in determining rate constants and intrinsic chemical properties, which are important for theoretical advancements and practical applications in chemical engineering [4].

The model building process is methodical and iterative, beginning with the fitting of a comprehensive initial model, followed by subsequent refinement through diagnostic analysis, possible data transformation, exploration of all possible regressions, and thorough evaluation of the best-performing models. To enhance the reliability of the analysis, an additional step for outlier detection has been integrated into the established workflow, addressing the anomalies inherent in empirical data.

The paper will proceed by detailing the dataset and the methodologies employed, presenting the results of the analysis, and discussing the conclusions drawn from the study. By doing so, it aims to demonstrate the rough process of predictive modeling within the chemical engineering domain.

Dataset

The dataset was generated from a chemical reaction in a tube reactor [1]. NbOCl3NbOCl_3NbOCl3​ was carried by an inert gas into the reactor where it reacted with COCl2COCl_2COCl2​ [1]. The products and unreacted NbOCl3NbOCl_3NbOCl3​ were condensed, and the remaining gases were analyzed to measure CO2CO_2CO2​ concentrations, indicative of NbOCl3NbOCl_3NbOCl3​ conversion [1]. Variables recorded included concentrations of NbOCl3NbOCl_3NbOCl3​ and COCl2COCl_2COCl2​, with the NbOCl3NbOCl_3NbOCl3​ concentration determined in part by mol density and mol fraction of CO2CO_2CO2​. The full reaction is given below[1]:

NbOCl3+COCl2=NbCl5+CO2 NbOCl_3+COCl_2=NbCl_5+CO_2 NbOCl3​+COCl2​=NbCl5​+CO2​

Response Variable:

  • yyy: The concentration of NbOCl3NbOCl_3NbOCl3​.

Regressors:

  • x1x_1x1​: The concentration of phosgene COCl2COCl_2COCl2​. This variable is important as it is a reactant in the reaction, and its concentration is expected to directly influence the formation of the products.

  • x2x_2x2​: Spacetime, a variable that encompasses the volumetric flow rate and the tube reactor volume.

  • x3x_3x3​: The molar density of the product gas.

  • x4x_4x4​: The molar fraction of carbon dioxide CO2CO_2CO2​ in the product gas.

Methods

The regression analysis conducted in this study was performed using the Python library statsmodels. The process began with fitting the full model to the collected data. The quality of the fit was initially assessed by examining the summary output provided by statsmodels, which included the R2R^2R2 value as a measure of the model's explanatory power. Alongside R2R^2R2, the output displayed the estimated coefficients for each predictor and their statistical significance, indicating the impact of each regressor on the response variable.

Subsequent to the initial fit, a series of diagnostic plots were generated to evaluate the assumptions underlying linear regression. This included plotting the residuals against each regressor to detect any patterns that might suggest non-linear relationships. Residuals were also plotted against the predicted values to check for homoscedasticity. The normal probability plot, another critical diagnostic tool, was used to assess the normality of the residuals, a key assumption of linear regression.

Further diagnostics involved the use of leverage and influence plots, which help in identifying outliers with a disproportionate impact on the model. Finally, the predicted values were plotted against the actual values to visually assess the model's predictive accuracy. The closer these points lay to the line y=xy = xy=x, the more precise the model is considered to be in predicting the dependent variable. Finally, VIF was calculated for each regressor for the best performing model to investigate collinearity.

Through this three-phase methodical approach, each phase corresponding to the repetetive loop in the flowchart depicted above, the study aimed to ensure that the selected model not only fit the data well but also adhered to the assumptions required for linear regression analysis. For some phases, for the sake of brevity, only a few graphs are actually displayed. Full python code accompanies this paper.

Conclusions

The theoretical model stands as the best of our modeling efforts, aligning closely with the principles of chemical kinetics. With the highest RPred2R^2_{Pred}RPred2​ of any model investigated thus far, it does the best job at predicting future observations. The theoretical model best fits the assumptions of least squares regression as all of the residual plots appear to be homoskedastic. There does not appear to be any significant outliers per the influence plot, and the normal probability plot appears nominal. However, VIF for some regressors is high (VIF>10VIF>10VIF>10), but this could potentially be overlooked as some collinearity is expected when recording data related to a physical process theoretically governed by equations and physical principles.

While the previous models and then theoretical model provided valuable insights, the omission of temperature likely contributed to unaccounted variability. In chemical kinetics, temperature is a vital factor influencing reaction rates, as described by the Arrhenius equation [3]:

k=Ae−EaRT k = A e^{-\frac{E_a}{RT}} k=Ae−RTEa​​

where:

  • kkk is the rate constant
  • AAA is the pre-exponential factor
  • EaE_aEa​ is the activation energy
  • RRR is the universal gas constant
  • TTT is the temperature in Kelvin

The rate constant kkk in the Arrhenius equation is exponentially related to the inverse of temperature, signifying that even slight variations can have significant effects on reaction rates. The rate constant is already included in our model through the intercept term, which can be interpreted as encompassing the effects of temperature on the reaction rate. Consequently, the theoretical model not only fits the observed data but can also be adapted to include temperature as a regressor.

This study's integration of linear regression analysis with theoretical chemical knowledge is a classic example of chemical engineering practices [4]. This type of comprehensive analysis can be employed to find out rate constants or orders of reactions given a chemical equation, as studies can be structured to produce data necessary for this kind of investigation to take place. As an example, the coefficient for the ln(x1)ln(x_1)ln(x1​) term in the theoretical model is very close to 111, potentially indicating that the concentration of reactant COCl2COCl_2COCl2​ enters the rate equation with an order of 1.

For the purpose of this linear regression analysis, further models may be developed by incorporating temperature to further refine the predictive capabilities of the model.
